Wrap Up

Next Steps

What should you do next? Well, you know what they say, practice makes permanent. As you've worked through this course, we hope you've started building your own Elixir app. That's a great way to cement what you've learned in this course and also gain more experience with Elixir and OTP!

Recommended Resources

  • Follow @elixirlang on Twitter for official Elixir news.

  • Subscribe to the Elixir Radar weekly newsletter for Elixir-related news, handpicked by the company that created Elixir, Plataformatec.

  • Stay tuned to the official Elixir blog for Elixir release information.

  • As a refresher and for quick reference, read through the official Elixir Guides. They're written by Elixir's creator, José Valim, so you get his unique perspective and insights.

  • Skim through the Standard Library Documentation. Reading the top-level description of each module gives you a sense of what's built in to Elixir so you know what's possible out-of-the-box and don't end up reinventing a wheel.

  • Search for external Elixir and Erlang libraries on Hex.

  • Follow discussions on the cocommunity-driven Elixir Forum.

  • In terms of Elixir/OTP books, we heartily recommend owning a copy of the following:

    • Elixir in Action by Saša Jurić
    • The Little Elixir & OTP Guidebook by Benjamin Tan Wei Hao
    • Programming Elixir by Dave Thomas

  • Now that you're familiar with Elixir and OTP, getting comfortable with the Phoenix web framework isn't a big hurdle! For a good jump start, we recommend the excellent Phoenix Guides.

Unpack A Multi-Player Bingo Game

How would you design a full-stack application that takes advantage of:

  • the concurrency and fault-tolerance of Elixir/OTP,
  • the real-time messaging of Phoenix channels,
  • and the friendly reliability of Elm on the front-end?

That's what our Unpacked: Multi-Player Bingo video series is all about!

We explore the design and code of a web app that lets multiple players compete at buzzword bingo in real time. We unpack a completed app layer by layer, focusing less on the syntax and more on the design.

By understanding how it all works together, you can combine Elixir, Phoenix, and Elm to create some very cool applications!

Most important, put your new skills to work right away... and have fun doing it!
